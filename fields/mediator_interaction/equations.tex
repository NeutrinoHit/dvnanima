\section*{Interaction Through a Mediator Field}

We use two scalar fields in $(2+1)$ dimensions: the main field $\phi(t,x,y)$ and the mediator field $\chi(t,x,y)$.

The starting Lagrangian is
\[
\mathcal L=
\frac12\partial_\mu\phi\,\partial^\mu\phi
+\frac12\partial_\mu\chi\,\partial^\mu\chi
-V(\phi,\chi),
\]
with
\[
V(\phi,\chi)=
\frac12 m_\phi^2\phi^2
+\frac12 m_\chi^2\chi^2
+g\,\chi\phi^2
+\frac{\lambda_\phi}{4}\phi^4
+\frac{\lambda_\chi}{4}\chi^4.
\]

The full equations of motion are
\[
\Box\phi+m_\phi^2\phi+2g\,\phi\chi+\lambda_\phi\phi^3=0,
\qquad
\Box\chi+m_\chi^2\chi+g\,\phi^2+\lambda_\chi\chi^3=0,
\qquad
\Box=\partial_t^2-\partial_x^2-\partial_y^2.
\]

Equivalently,
\[
\partial_t^2\phi-\nabla^2\phi+m_\phi^2\phi=-2g\,\phi\chi-\lambda_\phi\phi^3,
\qquad
\partial_t^2\chi-\nabla^2\chi+m_\chi^2\chi=-g\,\phi^2-\lambda_\chi\chi^3.
\]

The quartic couplings are chosen positive so that the potential is bounded from below:
\[
\lambda_\phi>0,\qquad \lambda_\chi>0.
\]

Initial data:

\begin{itemize}
\item $\phi(0,x,y)$ is taken as two incoming Gaussian wave packets with opposite carrier momenta and nonzero impact parameter;
\item by default, $\chi(0,x,y)=0$ and $\dot\chi(0,x,y)=0$, so the mediator field is generated dynamically by the source $-g\phi^2$;
\item an optional linearized static initialization can be used for smoother starts, but the main evolution solves the full nonlinear two-field system.
\end{itemize}

Interpretation:

\begin{itemize}
\item $\phi$ creates a source proportional to $\phi^2$;
\item $\chi$ then propagates as a genuine dynamical field;
\item the resulting $\chi$ profile acts back on $\phi$ through the term $-2g\,\phi\chi$;
\item the quartic self-interactions stabilize the dynamics against runaway growth.
\end{itemize}
